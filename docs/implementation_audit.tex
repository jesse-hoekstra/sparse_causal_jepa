\section{Data: the bounce environment}
\label{sec:data}
File: \code{src/scjepa/data/} (\code{BounceDataset}). Replicates Baumgartner App.~E
``Bounce'' as closely as their paper specifies \paper{Baum.\ App.~E}: $N=5$ elastic balls in
a box, object-level (state) observations, masses drawn per episode
\[
  m_i \sim \mathcal N(1.5,\,0.5^2)\ \text{clamped to}\ [0.5,3],
  \qquad r_i = r_0\, m_i / \bar m_{\mathrm{ref}},
\]
with $r_0=0.08$ and $\bar m_{\mathrm{ref}}$ the \emph{episode-independent} normal mean
(radius proportional to mass, as in their setup). \dev{}~Their exact mass distribution is
unspecified upstream; ours is a declared choice (\code{data.mass\_normal}, decisions D13).
The episode-independent radius reference is fix G1 (decisions D16 amendments): normalizing
radii by the \emph{episode} mean made absolute mass unidentifiable by any model.

Each item also carries \code{contacts} $(B,L-1,N,N)$: which ball pairs are in contact
during each transition, including wall bounces on the diagonal iff radius depends on mass
(fix G2) --- these define the ground-truth \emph{local} graphs of
Section~\ref{sec:eval}.

Context length $T_h=30$ \dev{}: Baumgartner's parameter encoder $p_\phi(\hat\theta|\tau)$
conditions on the \emph{full} trajectory \paper{Baum.\ p.~6, p.~22}; our JEPA pools $\Sph$
from the context window only (that architectural difference \emph{is} the experiment). At
the measured contact rate of $0.1425$ per ball per step, $T_h=30$ gives
$1-(1-0.1425)^{30}\approx 99\%$ of ball-slots at least one collision inside the context ---
mass evidence for $\Sph$ ($T_h=10$ left $\approx22\%$ of slots mass-blind; D16 amendment
2026-07-13).

\section{Architecture (states regime)}
\label{sec:arch}
File: \code{src/scjepa/models/state\_jepa.py}. The vision-regime SAVi encoders are replaced
by two \emph{separate} per-object linear embeddings (D9 mirrored):
\[
  \tilde S_{t} = W_c\, x_t + b_c \in \mathbb{R}^{N\times d}
  \quad\text{(context branch)},\qquad
  S^{\mathrm{tgt}}_{t} = W_g\, x_t + b_g
  \quad\text{(target branch)},
\]
applied frame-wise and shared across objects, so slot $i \equiv$ object $i$ by construction
(no alignment problem; this regime exists to isolate the channel-split + SPARTAN question
from encoder quality, \paper{my\_paper experiment 1}, \paper{SPARTAN App.~D}).

\subsection{Channel split: $\Sph$ and the kinematic anchors (D4/D14)}
File: \code{src/scjepa/models/channel\_split.py}. Two heads read the context history
$\tilde S \in \mathbb R^{B\times T_h\times N\times d}$:

\paragraph{Causal parameters (cross-slot temporal attention pooling, D14 default).}
One query per slot, projected from that slot's \emph{last} context step (the identity
anchor); keys/values are \emph{all} $T_h\!\cdot\!N$ history tokens plus a learned temporal
positional encoding (deliberately \emph{no} slot-identity PE, preserving slot-permutation
equivariance):
\[
  q_i = W_q\,\tilde s_i^{T_h},\qquad
  \mathrm{KV} = \{\tilde s_j^{k} + p_k\}_{j\le N,\,k\le T_h},\qquad
  \hat s^{\mathrm{ph}}_i = \mathrm{LN}\!\big(u_i + \mathrm{MLP}(u_i)\big),\;
  u_i = \mathrm{LN}\!\big(q_i + \mathrm{MHA}(q_i,\mathrm{KV},\mathrm{KV})\big).
\]
$\Sph$ is pooled \textbf{once per episode} from the context window and reused, fixed, at
every rollout step --- the premise of the identifiability theory
\paper{my\_paper p.~7/p.~16}, \paper{Baum.\ Lemma~1, p.~25}. Cross-slot (rather than
per-slot, D4) evidence is required because $m_i$ is only observable through the partner's
velocity change at contact (D13 caveat).

\paragraph{Kinematic anchors.} A learned linear map on per-step slots:
$S_t = W_k\,\tilde s^{(t)} + b_k$ (\code{KinematicHead.project}). Anchors are taken at the
\emph{true} embedded states at steps $t = T_h\!-\!1 + cT_p$ for chains $c = 0,\dots$
(one chain at $T_p{=}30$).

\subsection{SPARTAN predictor}
File: \code{src/scjepa/models/spartan.py}. Implemented from the paper alone (no public
code). Token set per call: $T = 2N$ tokens
$[\,S\ \text{tokens } 1..N \mid \Sph\ \text{tokens } N{+}1..2N\,]$, projected
$d \to d_e = 128$ (\paper{SPARTAN App.~A.1} separates token dim from a larger working
dim), plus \emph{learned role embeddings} added to state vs.\ parameter tokens
\interp{} (roles must be distinguishable while keeping slot symmetry; D10).

\paragraph{Gates.} \paper{SPARTAN Eq.~3}, per layer $l$:
\[
  A^{(l)}_{ij} \sim \mathrm{Bern}\big(\sigma(\ell^{(l)}_{ij})\big),
  \qquad
  \ell^{(l)}_{ij} = \frac{q_i^\top k_j}{\sqrt{d_e}}.
\]
\dev{}~The paper prints \emph{unscaled} $q^\top k$; at $d_e=128$ and standard init that is
$\sim\mathcal N(0,4^2)$, gates are born saturated and the Eq.~11 penalty starts at $e^{10}$
(observed catastrophic run \code{n5zq9nct}). We read $q^\top k$ as the conventionally
scaled attention logit --- the only init-trainable reading (D10 flag in the file header).

Sampling is the binary Gumbel-softmax \paper{SPARTAN p.~5, ref.\ [17]}: with logistic noise
$\varepsilon$ (= difference of two Gumbels),
\[
  \mathrm{soft} = \sigma\!\big((\ell + \varepsilon)/T_{\mathrm{gs}}\big),\qquad
  A_{ij} = \mathbf{1}[\mathrm{soft} > \tfrac12] \;\;(\equiv \mathbf{1}[\ell > -\varepsilon]),
\]
forward exactly hard $\{0,1\}$, backward straight-through via
$A + \mathrm{soft} - \mathrm{sg}(\mathrm{soft})$. Temperature
$T_{\mathrm{gs}}=1$, unannealed; note $T_{\mathrm{gs}}$ cancels from the \emph{decision}
(only scales the ST gradient). Eval mode is deterministic, $A_{ij} = \mathbf{1}[\ell_{ij}>0]$
\interp{} (the paper does not state its eval procedure).

\textbf{D19 gate-noise coupling} \dev{}: within one rollout chain the noise $\varepsilon$
is drawn \textbf{once per layer per chain} (\code{Spartan.sample\_gate\_noise}) and reused
at all $T_p$ steps; logits recompute each step. Hence at every step, marginally,
$\Pr(A_{ij}{=}1) = \sigma(\ell_{ij})$ exactly as Eq.~3, but within a chain a gate flips
only when its state-dependent logit crosses the chain's fixed threshold. Across chains the
draws are independent. Rationale (decisions D19): no source ever backpropagates through
more than one sampling round --- SPARTAN's objective is single-transition
\paper{SPARTAN Eq.~6}, Baumgartner's decoder ``performs one-step prediction''
\paper{Baum.\ Fig.~1 caption} --- so the cross-step coupling is unprescribed; the i.i.d.\
coupling detonates gradients at mid density under $T_p{=}30$ BPTT (three dead runs +
reproduction), the common-threshold coupling trains through.

\paragraph{Masked attention.} \paper{SPARTAN Eq.~4}, with the softmax renormalized over
\emph{unmasked entries only} \interp{} (masking after normalization would leak masked
tokens through the denominator, contradicting the paper's ``adjacency disallows
information flow''; D10):
\[
  h_i = \sum_j \frac{A_{ij}\exp(\ell_{ij})}{\sum_{j'} A_{ij'}\exp(\ell_{ij'})}\; v_j,
  \qquad
  \text{output tokens} = \mathrm{MLP}(h + \text{tokens}) \;\;\big(\hat s_i = \mathrm{MLP}(h_i + s_i)\big).
\]
Numerical safeguards (both empirically load-bearing; see file comments):
row-wise max subtraction uses the \emph{unmasked} max (a masked global max collapses the
denominator to the floor and amplifies gradients $\sim\!10^6\times$); fully masked rows get
denominator $+1$ instead of an $\epsilon$ floor, yielding $h_i = 0$ with bounded ST
gradients (this also implements the identity mode cleanly).

\paragraph{Path matrix and sparsity.} \paper{SPARTAN Eq.~5, Eq.~6}:
\[
  \Abar = \big(A^{(L_s)} + I\big)\cdots\big(A^{(1)} + I\big),
  \qquad
  \text{sparsity} = \E_b\,\|\Abar\|_1 = \E_b \sum_{ij} \Abar_{ij},
\]
with $L_s = 2$ layers (\code{model.spartan\_layers}). $\Abar_{ij}$ counts \emph{paths}
$j\!\to\!i$ (entries are integers $\ge 0$; the diagonal always $\ge 1$ from the residual
$+I$). \code{path\_density} = fraction of entries $\ge 0.5$; an identity-only matrix gives
exactly $1/T = 0.1$ (the empty-graph floor and collapse signature).

\paragraph{Attention-logit penalty.} \paper{Baum.\ Eq.~11} (their F.4 ablation shows it is
required against path-loss plateaus):
\[
  L_{\mathrm{logit}} = \E_{ij}\big[\exp(\ell_{ij}) + \exp(-\ell_{ij})\big]
  \;\;\xrightarrow{\text{impl.}}\;\;
  \E_{ij}\big[e^{c_{ij}} + e^{-c_{ij}} + e^{c_{ij}}(|\ell_{ij}|-30)_+\big],
  \quad c_{ij} = \min(|\ell_{ij}|,30),
\]
i.e.\ exact within $|\ell|\le 30$ and linearly continued beyond \dev{} (slope $e^{30}$:
an effectively hard, fp32-finite wall; the earlier $|\ell|\le10$ clamp was soft enough for
task gradients to push logits to $\sim$100 --- run \code{rung1\_seed0}). Averaged over
layers.

\paragraph{Reference modes.} \code{dense=True}: $A \equiv 1$, no sampling --- SPARTAN's
``fully connected model'' \paper{SPARTAN p.~16}, the $\tau$ reference (audit F-8: the gated
model with sparsity off is \emph{not} that reference; its gates keep sampling).
\code{identity=True}: $A \equiv 0$, so $\Abar = I$ --- each state token predicted from its
own past only, structurally mass-blind; our go/no-go reference (D16/D17). Mutually
exclusive; neither samples noise.

\section{Training objective}
\label{sec:objective}
File: \code{src/scjepa/training/loop.py} (\code{Trainer.\_train\_step}).

\subsection{Autoregressive rollout (D16)}
\dev{}~w.r.t.\ both papers' \emph{training} procedures (single-/one-step; see D19 notes
above), but paper-\emph{literal} w.r.t.\ the theory both share: the trajectory as repeated
composition with one fixed parameter,
\[
  S_{T_p} = \big[\,S_t,\; f(S_t,\Sph),\; (f\circ f)(S_t,\Sph),\;\dots\big]
  \qquad \paper{my\_paper p.~7/p.~16},\ \paper{Baum.\ Eq.~27}.
\]
Implementation (\code{rollout\_predictions}, \code{src/scjepa/models/jepa.py}): chains are
anchored at true embedded states, then feed their own predictions back,
\[
  \hat S^{(0)} = S_t \ \text{(anchor)},\qquad
  \hat S^{(k+1)} = \mathrm{SPARTAN}\big(\hat S^{(k)},\, \Sph\big),\quad k = 0,\dots,T_p-1,
\]
reusing one $\Sph$ and (D19) one gate-noise draw for the whole chain. $T_p$ must divide
$K$; $T_p{=}1$ recovers the teacher-forced ablation (old D15 objective, kept only as
ablation --- under it, edges were worth $\sim$7\% of MSE and the empty graph satisfied any
realistic $\tau$; the v2 collapse). Note the anchor lives in kinematic-head space while
fed-back predictions live in target-embedding space; the predictive loss ties the two
spaces together (documented in the function).

\subsection{Loss terms}
\paragraph{Prediction.} Permutation-invariant MSE over Hungarian-matched slots
(\code{hungarian\_mse}, \code{src/scjepa/losses/predictive.py}):
\[
  L_{\mathrm{pred}} = \frac{1}{|\mathcal B|} \sum_{b}\;
  \min_{\pi \in \mathfrak S_N} \frac{1}{Nd}\big\|\hat S_b - (S_b)_{\pi}\big\|_2^2 ,
\]
over all $B\!\cdot\!K$ (prediction, target) pairs. In the states regime slots are aligned
by construction, so the matching is the identity in practice; it exists for the vision
regime (D6).

\paragraph{Anti-collapse regularizer (VISReg, D3).} Applied to \emph{both} branches'
slot embeddings, each flattened to samples $z \in \mathbb R^{S\times d}$
($S = B\,T_h N$ context; $B\,K N$ target):
\[
  L_{\mathrm{reg}}(z) = \underbrace{\|\bar z\|_2^2/d}_{\text{center}}
  + \underbrace{\tfrac1d\sum_{a=1}^{d}\big(\mathrm{std}_a(z) - 1\big)^2}_{\text{scale}}
  + \underbrace{\E_{w\sim \mathbb S^{d-1}}\big\|\mathrm{sort}(z_{\mathrm{norm}} w) - \Phi^{-1}\text{-quantiles}\big\|^2}_{\text{shape (sliced-Wasserstein to } \mathcal N(0,1))},
\]
vendored verbatim from the official repo (\code{third\_party/visreg}; $256$ random
projections). This is the \emph{only} collapse prevention: joint training, no EMA, no
stop-gradient (D7). The scale term ($\mathrm{std}\to1$) is the anchor against
constraint-satisfaction-by-shrinking (failure catalog \#3) --- and the reason raw MSE is
measured with a solution-dependent ruler (motivating D17 below).

\paragraph{Total gradient objective.} \paper{Baum.\ Eq.~10} adapted (we have no VAE, hence
no $L_{\mathrm{KL}}$; VISReg deliberately sits \emph{outside} the constraint):
\[
  L_{\mathrm{total}} = L_{\mathrm{pred}}
  + \lambda_{\mathrm{logit}} L_{\mathrm{logit}}
  + \lambda_{\mathrm{reg}}\big(L_{\mathrm{reg}}(\tilde S) + L_{\mathrm{reg}}(S^{\mathrm{tgt}})\big)
  + \tfrac{1}{\lambda}\,\|\Abar\|_1 \quad\text{(if sparsity enabled)}.
\]
The $1/\lambda$ weighting is SPARTAN's GECO rewrite \paper{SPARTAN App.~A.2}: ``$(MSE-\tau)
+ |\Abar|/\lambda$ \dots\ error first, pruning later.''

\subsection{The constraint and the dual (D17)}
The dual controller (\code{src/scjepa/training/lagrangian.py}) compares to $\tau$ \emph{not}
the raw prediction loss but the scale-free constraint \dev{} (the project's one deliberate
deviation from \paper{Baum.\ Eq.~9} as printed):
\[
  c \;=\; \frac{L_{\mathrm{pred}}}{\mathrm{sg}\!\big[\mathrm{Var}(S^{\mathrm{tgt}}_{\text{batch}})\big]\vee 10^{-6}}
  \;+\; \lambda_{\mathrm{logit}}\, L_{\mathrm{logit}},
  \qquad
  \mathrm{Var} = \tfrac1d\textstyle\sum_a \mathrm{Var}_a(\text{batch target slots}),
\]
with $L_{\mathrm{logit}}$ kept \emph{inside} the constraint exactly as Eq.~9 has it.
Rationale (D17): Eq.~9 implicitly assumes a fixed measurement space (Baumgartner:
observations; SPARTAN: frozen embeddings); our target space is trainable and different
models equilibrate at different scales (measured: identity ref std $\approx0.46$ vs dense
$\approx0.54$), so raw floors differ by only $0.008$ while normalized floors separate by
$\approx0.26$. Normalization only feeds the $\lambda$ update --- \textbf{gradients are
untouched} (no incentive to game the variance). Interpretation aid: $c = 1.0 \approx$
predicting the batch mean.

Dual update \paper{SPARTAN App.~A.2 / Baum.\ Eq.~10, GECO}: with momentum $0.99$
moving-average $\overline{c-\tau}$,
\[
  \log\lambda \;\leftarrow\; \mathrm{clip}\big(\log\lambda + \alpha\,\overline{c-\tau},\;
  [\log 10^{-3},\, \log 10^{6}]\big),\qquad \alpha = 10^{-3},
\]
i.e.\ $c > \tau \Rightarrow \lambda\uparrow \Rightarrow$ sparsity weight $1/\lambda$
$\downarrow$ (protect prediction, gates open); $c < \tau \Rightarrow$ pruning pressure
grows. $\alpha$ matches SPARTAN Fig.~5's $10^5$--$10^6$-step $\lambda$ timescales (audit
F-10; the old $0.02$ crossed the range in 2k steps and pruned before dynamics were
learned). $\lambda_{\max} = 10^6$ keeps the dual responsive after long constraint
violations.

\paragraph{$\tau$ protocol (D16/D17).} $\tau = 1.1\times$ the \emph{dense} reference's
converged held-out constraint $c$ \paper{SPARTAN p.~16: $\tau$ = FC model's loss}, where
the dense reference is $A\equiv1$, trained under the \emph{identical} config (D12) to a
\emph{comparable} length (undertrained references inflate $\tau$; v2 failure). Sanity
bound: $\tau$ must sit \emph{below} the mass-blind floor from the identity reference
($A\equiv0$; measured $\approx0.90$ at $T_p{=}30$, ctx~30), else the empty graph satisfies
the constraint and pruning collapses the graph (failure catalog \#2; \code{--tau-max}
guard in \code{run\_bounce\_example.sh}). Current calibration: dense floor
$0.531\pm0.016$ (run \code{kn8g9xgu}, flat 105k--178k) $\Rightarrow \tau = 0.584$;
gated-model logit share $\approx0.022$ is affordable inside the $\approx0.26$ normalized
window (it was fatal inside the $0.008$ raw one --- audit F-9).

\subsection{Optimizer robustness (D18)}
Adam, $\mathrm{lr}=3\times10^{-4}$ constant, global grad-clip at $1.0$. Guards, in order,
after \code{backward()}:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item Non-finite \emph{loss} $\Rightarrow$ raise (pre-existing guard).
  \item Pre-clip grad norm non-finite \emph{or} $> 10^3$ $\Rightarrow$ \textbf{skip} the
        batch entirely (no optimizer step, no dual update --- a pathological batch must
        not jolt the $\lambda$ EMA); counter \code{health/skipped\_steps}.
  \item $\ge 2000$ \emph{consecutive} skips $\Rightarrow$ raise (weights are frozen during
        skips, so patience is free; $\sim$8 epochs of all-spiking fresh draws = dead run,
        not bad luck). Rolling checkpoints \code{step\_<N>.pt} every 25k steps.
\end{enumerate}
Motivation: failure \#4 (finite $10^{30}$ loss passes \code{isfinite}; BPTT overflows to
$\infty$; \code{clip\_grad\_norm\_} multiplies all grads by $\max/\infty = 0$; silent
230k-step zombie).

\section{Evaluation harness}
\label{sec:eval}
File: \code{src/scjepa/eval/harness.py}; CLI \code{scripts/eval\_identifiability.py};
periodic during training every 5k steps on a held-out split (dataset seed offset 17),
model in eval mode (deterministic gates), under the \emph{same} rollout objective as
training.

\paragraph{Prediction metrics.} \code{pred\_loss} (raw Hungarian MSE),
\code{target\_var}, \code{pred\_loss\_normalized}, and
\code{constraint\_loss} $= c$ exactly as the dual measures it (mean of per-batch ratios)
--- $\tau$ is calibrated on \emph{this} number, never on bare \code{pred\_loss}.

\paragraph{Ground-truth local graphs.} From per-transition contacts
(\code{eval/graph.py}): in free flight the state graph is the diagonal; a contact between
$i$ and $j$ during the predicted transition adds state edges $i \leftrightarrow j$ and
parameter edges $m_j \to S_i$ for every contact participant (wall bounces contribute the
parameter \emph{self}-edge iff radius $\propto$ mass --- fix G2). One graph per transition
(local graphs, \paper{Baum.\ \S B.6}; the bounce environment is chosen precisely because
no single local graph identifies the masses).

\paragraph{Learned graphs and SHD.} Threshold the path matrix at $0.5$; state block
$\Abar[:N,:N]$, parameter block $\Abar[:N,N{:}2N]$. \code{shd\_state} / \code{shd\_param}
= mean structural Hamming distance to the ground-truth graphs, per transition. A
\emph{constant} \code{shd\_param} over evals $\equiv$ zero learned parameter edges (both
historical collapses).

\paragraph{MCC.} \paper{Baum.\ App.~F.1}: for every pair $(i,j)$ fit a one-hidden-layer
MLP (width 32, tanh) regressing true $m_i$ on learned $\hat\theta_j$ over a 90\% split;
score $R^2$ on the held-out 10\%;
$\mathrm{MCC} = \mathrm{mean}_i \max_j R^2_{ij}$ (reported as \code{mcc};
\code{mcc\_linear} is the fast Pearson mean-max proxy). Reference: their Fig.~3 bounce
SPARTAN $\approx0.9$; MCC ramps \emph{late} (their Fig.~17) --- flat-low is normal only
while density/SHD still move. The recovery grid (\code{recovery\_grid.png}) is their
Fig.~5/12 analogue: true $m_i$ vs learned parameter of slot $j$ at the best dimension;
identification = sharp diagonal.
